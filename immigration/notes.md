## Process 1
The process “family petition for a foreign spouse” will be carried out.
    
1. Get legally married.
2. Matt files Form I-130 (Petition for Alien Relative). During this process, evidence of the genuine relationship and documents from both parties are submitted.
3. Wait for I-130 approval. Estimated processing time: 10 to 18 months (constantly varies). Once USCIS approves the petition, the case is transferred to the NVC (National Visa Center).
4. Consular process: The NVC requests:
Payment of fees
- Form DS-260
- Form I-864 (Affidavit of Support)
- Civil documents:
- birth certificate
- police/background records
- marriage certificate
- passport
5. Demonstrate financial solvency: for a household of 2 people, approximately $25,000–$27,000 USD annually is usually required (may change each year).
6. Medical examination (Luz only).
7. Attend the interview at the Mexican consulate (Luz only).
8. Wait for the immigrant visa.

## Process 2

1. Apply for a K-1 visa: File Form I-129F.
2. Wait for approval. Estimated processing time: 8 to 15 months.
3. Consular interview: The Mexican fiancée attends the interview. If approved, she receives the K-1 visa.
4. Enter the United States: After entry, the couple must marry within 90 days.
5. Adjustment of status: After the marriage, file Form I-485 for the Green Card.

## **Option 1: Get Married First and Then Apply for Residency (CR-1 / IR-1)**

This is usually the most stable and recommended option if they can marry outside the U.S. or if the Mexican partner can legally enter the U.S. as a visitor without immediate immigrant intent.

### Step 1. Get Legally Married

They can marry:
- In Mexico
- In the United States
- In another country  

The marriage certificate must be official and legally valid.
---
### Step 2. The U.S. Citizen Files the Family Petition

The U.S. citizen spouse files:
- Form I-130 (Petition for Alien Relative)

Through:
- Official USCIS Website

Common Documents:
- Marriage certificate
- Passports of both spouses
- Proof of U.S. citizenship
- Photos together
- Evidence of a genuine relationship:
- messages
- travel records
- family photos
- call logs
- shared accounts
---
### Step 3. Wait for I-130 Approval

Estimated processing time:
- 10 to 18 months (varies frequently)
Once USCIS approves the petition:
- the case is transferred to the National Visa Center (NVC)
---

### Step 4. Consular Processing

The NVC will request:
- Fee payments
- Form DS-260
- Affidavit of Support (Form I-864)
- Civil documents:
- birth certificate
- police records
- marriage certificate
- passport
---
### Step 5. Financial Sponsorship Requirement

The U.S. citizen spouse must prove sufficient income.

Generally:
- around 125% of the federal poverty guideline
- for a household of 2 people, usually about $25,000–$27,000 USD annually (may change each year)

If income is insufficient:  
- a joint sponsor may be used
---

### Step 6. Medical Examination

The Mexican applicant must attend a medical exam with embassy-approved doctors.
---

### Step 7. Consular Interview

Usually conducted at:
- U.S. Consulate General Ciudad Juárez
- Typical questions include:
  - how they met
  - details about the relationship
  - future plans together
  - immigration history
---

### Step 8. Receive the Immigrant Visa

Upon entering the United States:
- the Mexican spouse automatically becomes a lawful permanent resident
- The Green Card is mailed afterward.

<BR>

**Conditional or Permanent Residency**

If the couple has been married for less than 2 years:
- conditional residency valid for 2 years (CR-1)

Later they must remove conditions by filing:  
- Form I-751  

If married for more than 2 years:  
- permanent 10-year residency (IR-1)

## **Option 2: K-1 Fiancé(e) Visa**
This option is for couples who want to marry inside the United States.

Requirements
- The man must be a U.S. citizen
- Both must be legally free to marry
- They must have met in person at least once within the last 2 years

<BR>

### Step 1. File the K-1 Visa Petition
Submit:
- Form I-129F
---  

### Step 2. Wait for Approval

Estimated timeline:
- 8 to 15 months
---

### Step 3. Consular Interview

The Mexican fiancé(e) attends the visa interview.

If approved:
- the K-1 visa is issued
---

### Step 4. Enter the United States

After entering:
- the couple must marry within 90 days
---

### Step 5. Adjustment of Status

After marriage:
- file Form I-485 for the Green Card

The applicant may also apply for:
- work authorization
- travel authorization
